# Poyar — Usage-Based Pricing

**TL;DR:** Usage-based pricing (UBP) aligns price to delivered value and drives best-in-class net revenue retention, but only when the value metric, packaging, and expansion mechanics are designed together.

## Core principles
- **UBP correlates with higher NRR.** Top UBP companies routinely post NRR well north of 120%; revenue expands automatically as customers succeed, without a renewal renegotiation.
- **Land-and-expand without a sales touch.** Consumption growth *is* the expansion motion — the account grows as usage grows, which is the PLG monetization flywheel.
- **The value metric is the whole game.** It must track customer-perceived value, be predictable enough to budget, and scale with the customer's own success (e.g. API calls, rows synced, compute, messages, GB).
- **Hybrid is the pragmatic default.** A platform/commitment fee plus a consumption component gives revenue predictability (for finance) and value alignment (for the customer) — pure pay-as-you-go scares procurement.
- **Free tiers and credits are acquisition levers**, but only convert if the value metric and usage limits are tuned so real value sits just past the free ceiling.

## How it informs our product
- UBP/hybrid accounts are exactly our **ICP** (see [[ideal-first-design-partner]]) — leakage and expansion both live in the consumption line.
- **Expansion mechanics are measurable:** NRR, expansion %, and contraction % become first-class metrics; the platform attributes expansion to usage growth vs. upsell vs. price.
- **Value-metric fit checks:** flag accounts where consumption stagnates or where discounting on the platform fee masks weak usage economics.
- Quote/deal guidance can recommend **commitment-tier structures** (ramps, prepaid credits) that protect price realization while preserving consumption upside.

## Watch-outs / where it's weak
- Benchmarks are aggregate and survivorship-biased — median UBP companies do not all hit elite NRR.
- Pure consumption pricing creates revenue volatility and budgeting anxiety for buyers; misaligned metrics can punish customers for using the product more.
