# Campbell — Willingness to Pay & Value Metrics

**TL;DR:** Monetization is a continuous, data-driven discipline. Quantify willingness to pay (WTP) at scale, pick a value metric that scales with delivered value, and treat pricing as something you revisit constantly — not a one-time launch decision.

## Core principles
- **Measure WTP quantitatively and often.** Use survey methods (Van Westendorp price-sensitivity meter, max-diff / conjoint for feature value) on real customers to find acceptable price ranges and feature value — at scale, not anecdotally.
- **The value metric is the most important pricing decision.** Charge along the axis that grows with customer-perceived value (e.g. contacts, seats-used, events) so price rises as the customer succeeds.
- **Segment by buyer persona.** WTP and the right value metric differ sharply across personas; a single price leaves money on the table and misfits parts of the market.
- **Pricing is a process, not a project.** Revisit price and packaging on a cadence; the most under-optimized lever in SaaS is the one teams touch least.
- **Price increases vs. churn is a measurable tradeoff.** Most teams under-price out of churn fear; the churn/contraction impact of a well-targeted increase is usually smaller than expected and net-positive — but it must be tested, not assumed.

## How it informs our product
- **Value-metric selection** is a core capability — we diagnose whether the partner's metric tracks delivered value or invites leakage and friction.
- WTP distributions give the **segment-level reference points** that complement Nagle's EVC, sharpening discount-band and price-realization analysis.
- The **price-increase / churn tradeoff** is directly modelable in the elasticity & scenario pillar (#3) — simulate revenue net of expected contraction before a partner moves price.
- Reinforces "pricing is a system not a number" — the platform is the cadence engine that makes continuous re-pricing operational.

## Watch-outs / where it's weak
- Stated-preference surveys (Van Westendorp especially) measure *claimed* WTP and skew optimistic — anchor against actual closed-deal behavior, which our diagnostic provides.
- Campbell's benchmarks come largely from SMB/PLG SaaS; enterprise, sales-led, usage-based deals behave differently — adjust, don't transplant.
