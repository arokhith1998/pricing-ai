# Rivera — Packaging Architecture

**TL;DR:** How you package and bundle is as decisive as the price itself. Most B2B SaaS leaves money on the table through sloppy packaging — overlapping tiers, mis-fenced features, and no link between packaging and the value metric. Audit it, then redesign deliberately.

## Core principles
- **Packaging is a first-class lever, distinct from price.** Tier structure, feature allocation, and bundling steer customers toward the right plan and protect margin before any number is negotiated.
- **Architect good/better/best with intent.** Each tier needs a clear target segment, a differentiating "killer" feature that justifies the step up, and clean fences so customers self-select and upgrade naturally.
- **Packaging must align to the value metric.** Tiers and add-ons should map to how value is delivered and measured (Campbell/Poyar) — otherwise discounting becomes the only way to fit the customer.
- **Run a monetization audit before redesigning.** Inventory current packages, prices, discounts, and realized economics to expose where the structure leaks (cannibalization, free riders, undifferentiated tiers).
- **Packaging transformation is change management.** Migration paths, grandfathering, and sales enablement matter as much as the new design.

## How it informs our product
- The **monetization audit IS our retrospective diagnostic** — packaging analysis is a natural extension of leakage analysis (a leak is often a packaging defect, not a rep behavior).
- When **win-rate-by-discount-band** shows deep discounting concentrated in one tier, the platform should flag a **packaging/fence problem**, not just a discipline problem.
- Feeds the **price optimization & packaging pillars (#2, #3)**: recommend tier moves, add-on unbundling, or fence changes that lift price realization.
- Quote/deal guidance can steer reps to a **better-fit package** instead of a discount when scope and value mismatch.

## Watch-outs / where it's weak
- Practitioner heuristics, not peer-reviewed method — validate recommendations against the partner's actual cohort behavior.
- Re-packaging is disruptive: migration risk, churn, and sales confusion can outweigh gains if rushed; sequence behind a solid leakage/realization baseline.
