# Pricekeel — Brand Guide

_One page. Last updated 2026-05-26._

## The name

A ship's **keel** is the structural backbone that runs the length of the hull. It is what
keeps a vessel upright, stable, and tracking straight — what keeps it on an **even keel**.

Pricekeel is the keel for a company's pricing. It keeps margin steady, holds the line on
realization, and stops discount leakage from listing the boat. The wedge is a
discount-leakage diagnostic for usage-based SaaS: find where price is quietly draining away,
and correct course before it compounds.

The mark reflects this directly: a flat **waterline** with a **hull curve** beneath it — the
keel sitting below the surface, doing the steadying work you don't see.

## Taglines

1. **Keep your pricing on an even keel.**
2. **Stop discount leakage.**
3. **Pricing intelligence for usage-based SaaS.**

Use (1) for brand/hero, (2) for the diagnostic wedge, (3) for category clarity / SEO.

## Logo

- **Mark:** waterline (Keel Navy) over hull curve (Waterline Teal). Two strokes, nothing else.
- **Full lockup:** mark + wordmark. "Price" in Keel Navy, "keel" in Waterline Teal.
- **Dark backgrounds:** use `logo-full-dark.svg` — waterline and "Price" go White; hull and
  "keel" stay Waterline Teal.
- **Favicon:** mark on a Keel Navy rounded square, waterline in White.

### Do
- Keep clear space around the mark equal to the height of the waterline stroke.
- Use the provided SVGs at any scale; they are vector and crisp at all sizes.
- Use the dark variant on Keel Navy, photography, or any dark surface.

### Don't
- Don't recolor the wordmark or split the two-tone "Price" / "keel" treatment.
- Don't add gradients, shadows, bevels, or outlines to the mark.
- Don't rotate, stretch, skew, or crop the mark.
- Don't place the light logo on busy or low-contrast backgrounds.

## Typography

- **Primary:** Inter. Headings SemiBold (600). Body Regular (400).
- **Metrics & tables:** Inter with **tabular figures** (`font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums`)
  so numbers align in columns.
- **Code, IDs, quote numbers, SKUs:** **IBM Plex Mono**.
- Generous line height for dense financial tables; tight, deliberate headings.

## Voice & tone

Precise, senior, CFO-credible. We sound like the most trusted person in the room — the one
who quantifies the problem and the fix without theatrics.

- **Precise** — name the number, the band, the leakage. Specifics over adjectives.
- **Confident, not loud** — assert, then back it with evidence.
- **No hype** — no "revolutionary," "game-changing," "AI-powered magic." No exclamation marks.
- **No emoji.**
- **Plain over clever** — a CFO should grasp the value in one read.
- **Honest about uncertainty** — ranges and confidence over false precision.

**On-brand:** "You're leaking 6.4 points of realization in the 15–20% discount band. Tighten
approval thresholds there and recover ~$1.2M ARR."

**Off-brand:** "Our revolutionary AI supercharges your pricing! 🚀 Unlock explosive growth!"
